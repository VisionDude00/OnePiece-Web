## Character Spotlights

### Saint Jalmack (DEBUT)
- First appearance in this episode
- Introduced as one of the most despicable Celestial Dragons
- Casually murders a child for "crossing in front of his ship"
- Voice: Kazuya Ichijo (JP), no English VA listed

### Sabo
- Makes ultimate choice for freedom
- Apparent death (later revealed as survival)
- Age 10 in this episode
- Voice: Toru Furuya (JP), Morgan Lauré Garrett (EN)

### Dragon
- Demonstrates wind manipulation powers
- Rescues both Gray Terminal residents and Sabo specifically
- Doesn't know Sabo is Luffy's sworn brother
- Sets up dramatic irony

## Themes

### Freedom vs Oppression
- Sabo's pirate flag = symbol of freedom
- Celestial Dragon's bazooka = World Government crushing freedom
- Gray Terminal burning = nobility erasing poverty from view

### Class Inequality
- Nobles willing to burn poor alive for "appearances"
- Celestial Dragon's massive ship vs Sabo's tiny fishing boat
- Dramatic power imbalance

### Sacrifice and Courage
- Dadan and Ace stay to fight despite danger
- Dragon risks himself to save oppressed people
- Sabo chooses uncertain freedom over comfortable captivity

## Final Notes

Episode 502 represents one of the most emotionally impactful episodes in the Post-War Arc flashback sequence. The tragedy of Sabo's apparent death, combined with the cruelty of Saint Jalmack and the nobility's willingness to burn the poor alive, creates a powerful commentary on class inequality and oppression that defines One Piece's themes.

The dramatic irony of Dragon saving his son's sworn brother without knowing their connection adds depth to the narrative. The episode effectively demonstrates the Revolutionary Army's mission and values while setting up major payoffs in future episodes (663, 737).
