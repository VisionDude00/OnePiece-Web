# Episode 535 Research Notes

## Research Date
December 19, 2025

## Episode Overview
Episode 535 "Hordy's Onslaught! The Retaliatory Plan Set Into Motion!" is a pivotal episode in the Fish-Man Island Arc where Hody Jones and Vander Decken IX successfully invade Ryugu Palace, marking the beginning of the arc's main conflict.

## Key Research Findings

### Animation Credits
- Screenplay: Tomohiro Nakayama
- Art Director: Ryūji Yoshiike
- Animation: Eisaku Inoue
- Director: Katsumi Tokoro

### Manga Adaptation
- Adapts Chapter 616 "Anniversary for Revenge" (pages 2-17)
- Generally faithful to manga content with some extended scenes for pacing

### Character Debuts
- **Den**: Tom's younger brother, a Bering wolffish merman who appears in the Sea Forest
  - Provides exposition about fish-man genetics
  - Helps Franky locate the Thousand Sunny

### Techniques Debuting
1. **Gigantesco Mano** (Robin) - Mentioned but shown off-screen
   - Full on-screen debut comes later in Episode 556
   - Represents Robin's post-timeskip enhanced abilities

2. **Yabusame/Archer Shark** (Hody Jones) - First appearance
   - Fish-man karate technique using water droplets as projectiles
   - Visually similar to Arlong's techniques from earlier arcs
   - Demonstrates Hody's combat abilities

### Thematic Elements
The episode strongly emphasizes:
- **Inherited Hatred**: Hody represents hatred passed down without personal experience of oppression
- **Infiltration and Long-term Planning**: Hody's revelation about joining the Neptune Army solely for training
- **Obsession**: Vander Decken's possessive pursuit of Shirahoshi
- **Betrayal**: The shock of Hody being a former trusted soldier

### Production Notes
- This episode marks a change in eyecatcher format
- New eyecatcher features One Piece logo with Luffy's silhouette after commercial breaks
- Opening song: "We Go!" by Hiroshi Kitadani (episodes 517-590)
- No ending song (ending songs were abolished from episode 278 to 1071)

### Voice Acting Notes
Japanese cast standouts:
- Joji Nakata as Hody Jones - Brings menace and cold calculation
- Wataru Takagi as Vander Decken IX - Portrays obsessive, unhinged nature

English Funimation cast:
- Larry Brantley as Hody Jones
- Jim Foronda as Vander Decken IX

### Interesting Observations

1. **Oda's Intent**: The author specifically created Hody Jones to represent inherited hatred without rational basis
   - Controversial anime filler showing young Hody witnessing discrimination undermined this message
   - Manga version maintains that Hody has NO personal experience of human oppression

2. **Fish-Man Genetics**: Den's explanation about ancestral traits is important world-building
   - Explains why fish-man siblings can look completely different
   - Justifies Tom and Den's different appearances despite being brothers

3. **Prophecy Setup**: This episode continues building toward Madam Shyarly's prophecy
   - Luffy protecting Shirahoshi while palace is attacked
   - Sets up eventual confrontation and prophecy fulfillment

4. **Character Parallels**: Hody's Yabusame technique deliberately echoes Arlong
   - Both use fish-man karate water-based attacks
   - Visual callback to Arlong Park Arc (episodes 39-43)
   - Reinforces thematic connection between villains
