# Research Notes for Episode 346

## Episode Overview
Episode 346 "The Vanishing Straw Hat Crew! A Mysterious Swordsman Appears!" is a pivotal episode in the Thriller Bark arc that introduces two new General Zombies and escalates the conflict between the Straw Hats and Moria's forces.

## Key Research Findings

### Character Debuts
1. **Jigoro** - General Zombie #850 with Zoro's shadow
   - Legendary swordsman who killed 7,000 pirates in life
   - Known as "Jigoro of the Wind" from West Blue
   - Voiced by Kazuya Nakai (same as Zoro) creating interesting meta-textual layer
   - Physical appearance: 238 cm tall, bandages with nails, missing tooth
   - Demonstrates complete transfer of Zoro's techniques and some personality

2. **Tararan** - Spider-monkey hybrid General Zombie
   - Commander of the Spider Mice
   - Unique hybrid design combining simian and arachnid features
   - Characteristic speech pattern: starts sentences with "Monkey, Monkey"
   - Uses Spider Nettori Net ability

### Episode Structure
- Based on Chapter 452 "Jigoro of the Wind" (pages 2-19)
- Opening: "Jungle P" by 5050
- No ending theme (standard for episodes 279-1070)
- Air date: March 9, 2008 (Japan), September 30, 2014 (Funimation), May 14, 2016 (Toonami)

1. **Identity Theft** - The horror of having one's abilities used against allies
2. **Divide and Conquer** - Moria's systematic separation of the Straw Hats
3. **Legends and Legacy** - Past warriors reanimated with present strength

### Interesting Observations

1. **Voice Acting Meta-Reference**: Kazuya Nakai voices both Zoro and Jigoro, meaning he essentially plays against himself in battle scenes. This is a brilliant production choice that emphasizes the shadow/original relationship.

2. **Anime Pacing**: The anime expanded the General Zombies battle sequences compared to the manga, adding more choreography and action to fill the episode runtime. This is typical of the anime's approach during this era.

3. **Pandaman Easter Egg**: The recurring character Pandaman appears as a zombie in background shots, continuing the series' tradition of hiding him in episodes.

4. **Shadow Mechanics Revelation**: This episode provides crucial information about how shadows work:
   - Complete technique transfer (Jigoro uses exact Zoro moves)
   - Partial personality transfer (Zoro's pride about wounds)
   - Personality eventually fades with time/obedience to Moria
   - Shadows retain interpersonal dynamics (Jigoro vs Inuppe mirrors Zoro vs Sanji)

5. **Moria's Strategy**: Research revealed Moria uses bounty amounts to determine shadow-to-corpse matching:
   - High bounties → General Zombies (legendary corpses)
   - Low/no bounties → Regular zombies
   - This explains systematic capturing of Straw Hats
