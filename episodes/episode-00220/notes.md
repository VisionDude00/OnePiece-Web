# Research Notes for Episode 220

## Overview
Episode 220 "Was it Lost? Stolen? Who Are You?" is the first episode of the Ocean's Dream Arc, a 5-episode filler arc (episodes 220-224) based on the PlayStation game "One Piece: Ocean's Dream!" This arc does not exist in the manga and is completely anime-original content.

## Key Research Findings

### Episode Details
- **Air Date (Japan):** February 6, 2005
- **Air Date (US):** September 1, 2012 (FUNimation)
- **TV Rating:** 11.2% (ranked 6th in timeslot)
- **Arc:** Ocean's Dream Arc (first episode)
- **Saga:** Water 7 Saga (positioned between Long Ring Long Land and Water 7)

### Plot Summary
A mysterious boy named Drim, controlled by a seahorse creature named Noko, appears at night and uses hypnotic music to steal the memories of all sleeping Straw Hat crew members. Only Robin, who was awake reading, retains her memories. When the crew wakes up, they have no memory of each other or their adventures, reverting to their pre-crew personalities. Nami steals treasure and flees, Zoro swims away, and Robin struggles unsuccessfully to keep everyone together.

### New Characters
1. **Drim** - A young boy with lavender hair under Noko's hypnotic control
   - Voice: Chinami Nishimura (JP), Mary Morgan (EN)
   - Has no inherent powers, serves as Noko's agent

2. **Noko** - A sentient seahorse with memory-stealing abilities
   - Voice: Keiichi Sonobe (JP), Cris George (EN)
   - Abilities: Memory theft via hypnotic music, hypnosis through eye contact, illusions
   - Motivation: Wants to become a Sennenryu (Millennium Dragon)

### Character Reversions
- **Nami:** Reverts to pirate-hating cat burglar
- **Zoro:** Reverts to lone bounty hunter
- **Usopp:** Thinks about his children's gang
- **Sanji:** Disoriented about leaving Baratie
- **Chopper:** Frightened without memories of Dr. Kureha
- **Luffy:** Confused about being on a pirate ship
- **Robin:** Only one with memories (was awake)

### Thematic Elements
The episode explores:
1. **Memory and Identity** - How shared experiences shape who we are
2. **Character Development** - Shows growth by contrasting past and present selves
3. **Trust and Bonds** - The fragility of relationships without shared history
4. **Robin's Commitment** - Tests her dedication to the crew despite being newest member

## Notable Observations

1. **Robin's Central Role:** The choice to make Robin the only one with memories is narratively clever - she's the newest member so she has the least knowledge of everyone's backstories, creating an ironic challenge.

2. **Character Development Showcase:** The memory loss serves as an effective way to demonstrate how much characters have grown by showing who they used to be.

3. **Game Adaptation:** The arc's origin as a PlayStation game adaptation explains some of the plot choices and the standalone nature of the story.

4. **Millennium Dragon Connection:** Noko's desire to become a Sennenryu connects to the earlier Warship Island filler arc, creating continuity between filler episodes.

5. **No Major Canon Impact:** As filler, this episode has no impact on the main story. It can be safely skipped without missing canon content.
