# Research Notes for Episode 355

## Research Process

### Episode Overview
Episode 355 "Food, Nami and Shadows!! Luffy's Angry Counterattack" aired on May 25, 2008 in Japan and is part of the Thriller Bark Arc. This episode adapts Chapter 460 (pages 2-19) titled "Get Back Before Dawn!!"

### Key Research Findings

#### 1. Episode Structure
- The episode focuses on the Straw Hats splitting up to accomplish multiple objectives
- Main plot threads: rescuing Nami, retrieving shadows from Moria, recovering stolen supplies
- Introduction of Oars as the primary antagonist moving forward
- Perona's Negative Hollow demonstrated as a powerful defensive weapon

#### 2. Notable Anime Enhancements
- **Sanji's "Super Saiyan Mode"**: The anime significantly enhanced Sanji's rage scene with extended close-ups and flame effects. This wasn't as emphasized in the manga (Chapter 460), making it a notable anime-original enhancement. Fans immediately dubbed this "Sanji's Super Saiyan mode" due to the visual similarity to Dragon Ball transformations.
- This rage scene later inspired Sanji's post-timeskip "Hell Memories" technique (Episode 566) and the anime-only "Sexy Fire" technique in Film Z.

#### 3. World-Building Elements
- **Salt Purification**: Important lore revealed about salt containing trace amounts of sea water that can neutralize Devil Fruit powers, specifically purifying Moria's zombies.
- **Shadow Transfer Mechanics**: Episode establishes that shadows transfer personality and memories but NOT Devil Fruit abilities, which are tied to the physical body.
- **Zombie Control Issues**: Strong-willed shadows (like Luffy's) resist Moria's control and require "breaking" before they can be commanded.

#### 4. Voice Acting Note
- Oars is voiced by the same actors as Luffy (Mayumi Tanaka in Japanese, Colleen Clinkenbeard in English) but with a distorted/deepened effect to reflect that it's Luffy's shadow in a giant zombie body.

#### 5. Opening/Ending Songs
- Opening: "Jungle P" by 5050 (episodes 326-372)
- Ending: None (One Piece discontinued ending themes before the Thriller Bark arc began)
