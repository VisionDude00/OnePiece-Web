# Research Notes for Episode 223

## Episode Overview
**Title:** Zoro Bares His Fangs! A Savage Animal Stands in the Way!
**Japanese Title:** 牙をむくゾロ！立ちはだかった野獣！ (Kiba o muku Zoro! Tachihadakatta yajū)
**Air Date:** February 27, 2005 (Japan)
**Arc:** Ocean's Dream Arc (Episodes 220-224)
**Type:** Filler / Anime-only

### Key Findings

#### Production Notes
- **Final Episode for Junki Takegami:** This episode is historically significant as it marked the final screenwriting credit for Junki Takegami, who had been writing for One Piece since the beginning of the series in 1999
- **TV Rating:** Achieved 10.9% TV rating, ranking 5th for that week in Japan - solid performance for a filler episode
- **Production Team:** Animation directed by Kenji Yokoyama, episode directed by Hidehiko Kadota, with art direction by Miyuki Sato and Fumihiro Uchikawa

#### Video Game Connection
- The entire Ocean's Dream Arc is based on the PlayStation game "One Piece: Ocean's Dream!" released in 2003
- This makes it one of the few anime arcs directly adapted from a video game
- The game was also remade for Game Boy Advance as "One Piece: Dragon Dream!"
- Character Drim also appeared in the 2019 film "One Piece: Stampede" as a cameo

#### Character Analysis

**Noko (Main Antagonist):**
- Seahorse creature with Nemu Nemu no Mi (Sleep-Sleep Fruit)
- Abilities: Memory stealing through sound, hypnotic eye control, spectral illusions
- Motivation: Wanted to become a Sennenryu (Millennium Dragon) by becoming big and floating
- Voice actors: Keiichi Sonobe (JP), Cris George (EN)

**Drim:**
- Young boy with lavender hair, controlled by Noko through hypnosis
- Victim rather than villain - acted against his will
- Voice actors: Chinami Nishimura (JP), Mary Morgan (EN)

#### Plot Structure
Episode 223 serves as the penultimate episode of the arc, featuring:
1. Crew splitting up (Luffy vs. Zoro, others continue to objective)
2. Discovery of true culprit (Noko revealed, Drim exonerated)
3. Climactic battle at lakeside
4. Beginning of memory recovery
5. Crew reunification

### Reception Analysis

**Mixed Critical Response:**
- Some viewers appreciated the arc's compelling narrative and meaningful flashbacks
- Others criticized it as confusing with a "stupid opponent"
- Generally acknowledged as skippable filler that doesn't impact main story
- Contemporary 2008 reviews were more critical, while later retrospective reviews (2021-2022) were more positive

### Notable Details

#### Music Licensing
- The ending theme "Mirai Kōkai" by Tackey & Tsubasa was replaced with "Eternal Pose" for FUNimation releases due to licensing issues
- This is a common issue with J-pop songs in international anime releases

#### Opening Song Context
- "Kokoro no Chizu" (Map of the Heart) by BOYSTYLE
- Episodes 207-263 (57 episodes total)
- First One Piece opening to be broadcast in widescreen format
- Released November 17, 2004

#### Canon Comparison
- The Nemu Nemu no Mi is anime-only and non-canon
- However, similar memory manipulation appears in canon through Charlotte Pudding's Memo Memo no Mi
- This suggests the anime staff may have been exploring memory-based Devil Fruit concepts that Oda later incorporated differently
