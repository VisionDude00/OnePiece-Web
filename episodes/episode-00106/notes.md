# Research Notes for Episode 106

## Episode Overview
**Title:** Trap in a Desperate Situation! Breaking Into Rain Dinners
**Air Date:** March 24, 2002 (Japan)
**Arc:** Alabasta Arc

## Key Research Findings

### Major Reveals in This Episode

1. **Seastone Introduction**: This episode provides the first detailed explanation of Seastone (sea prism stone) in the anime. Smoker's jitte is revealed to be tipped with this rare mineral that nullifies Devil Fruit powers. This is a crucial world-building element that becomes very important throughout the series.

2. **Crocodile's Devil Fruit**: The Suna Suna no Mi is fully revealed and explained for the first time. While Crocodile's sand powers were hinted at earlier, this is the first time he explicitly names his fruit and demonstrates its Logia-type intangibility.

3. **Operation Utopia**: The true scope of Baroque Works' conspiracy is unveiled, setting up the climax of the arc.

4. **Pell's Debut**: Introduces one of Alabasta's strongest defenders and one of only five flying Devil Fruit users in the One Piece world.

### Character Debuts

- **Pell**: Royal Guardian of Alabasta, user of the Tori Tori no Mi, Model: Falcon
  - Voice Actor (JP): Kenji Nojima
  - Voice Actor (EN): Marc Diraison (Funimation)
  - Note: Becomes famous for his sacrifice in Episode 125 and controversial survival

### Location Debuts

- **Rain Dinners**: Crocodile's casino/secret headquarters in Rainbase
  - Based architecturally on Luxor Las Vegas according to fan analysis
  - Contains hidden prison with Seastone cages
  - Serves as the location where Operation Utopia is announced

### Fan Reception and Theories

#### Crocodile as Best Villain
- Widely discussed in the fan community as possibly the best One Piece villain
- Arguments for: intelligent planning, defeated Luffy multiple times, ruthless execution, memorable design
- Forum discussions compare him favorably to Doflamingo, though opinions are divided

#### Crocodile's Secret Past
- The "Ivankov theory" (that Crocodile was assigned female at birth and changed by Ivankov's powers) is extremely popular
- Evidence comes from later Impel Down arc, not this episode
- Remains unconfirmed by Oda

#### Pell's Future Survival
- This episode sets up Pell's later "sacrifice" in Episode 125
- His survival of that event is one of the most controversial moments in One Piece
- Many fans view it as a narrative weakness, though Oda's intent may have been to avoid deaths before Marineford

### World-Building Significance

This episode is particularly important for introducing Seastone as a concept. This becomes a recurring element throughout the series:
- Prison cells in Impel Down
- Marine weapons and restraints
- Naval ship hulls (to avoid Sea Kings)
- Later plot devices involving Devil Fruit user captures

The concept that all Devil Fruit powers have this universal weakness adds strategic depth to the power system.

### Cultural References

**Egyptian Mythology**: Pell's falcon form connects to Alabasta's Egyptian theming. In ancient Egyptian mythology, falcons were associated with Horus, the god of the sky and kingship. Pell's role as a guardian of royalty mirrors this symbolism.

**Casino Setting**: Rain Dinners represents the corruption hidden behind a glamorous facade, a common theme in crime fiction. The contrast between the public casino and the hidden prison below reflects the dual nature of Crocodile's operation.
