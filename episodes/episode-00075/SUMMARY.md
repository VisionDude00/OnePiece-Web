# Episode 75: A Hex on Luffy! Colors Trap!

## Quick Facts
- **Episode Number:** 75
- **Air Date (Japan):** August 12, 2001
- **Air Date (US):** August 25, 2009
- **Arc:** Little Garden
- **Saga:** Alabasta Saga
- **Manga Chapters:** 123-124

## Synopsis
Luffy attempts to rescue Zoro, Nami, and Vivi from Mr. 3's deadly wax trap but inadvertently speeds up the waxing process. Miss Goldenweek reveals her hypnotic Colors Trap ability, using painted symbols to control Luffy's actions and emotions. While Luffy is incapacitated, Usopp must face Mr. 5 and Miss Valentine alone to protect Karoo and buy time.

## Major Events
1. **Gomu Gomu no Tonkachi Debut** - Luffy uses wax as hammers to attack Mr. 3
2. **Colors Trap Revealed** - Miss Goldenweek demonstrates her paint-based hypnotic ability
3. **Five Colors Trap Techniques Debut**:
   - Uragiri no Kuro (Black of Betrayal)
   - Warai no Kiiro (Yellow of Laughter)
   - Togyu no Aka (Red of Bullfighting)
   - Kanashimi no Ao (Blue of Sadness)
   - Nagomi no Midori (Green of Soothing)
4. **Usopp's Solo Stand** - Faces dangerous enemies alone while Luffy is hypnotized
5. **Crew in Mortal Danger** - Zoro, Nami, and Vivi nearly completely encased in wax

## Key Character Moments

### Luffy
- Debuts Gomu Gomu no Tonkachi technique
- Shows combat creativity but vulnerability to psychological attacks
- Falls victim to multiple Colors Trap effects
- Has a peaceful tea party while friends are dying

### Miss Goldenweek
- Reveals her Colors Trap ability (NOT a Devil Fruit power)
- Demonstrates multiple hypnotic techniques
- Completely neutralizes Luffy without physical combat
- Establishes herself as a tactical genius

### Usopp
- Character development: fights alone against Mr. 5 and Miss Valentine
- Protects Karoo when Luffy can't help
- Shows growing bravery despite fear

## Abilities Introduced
- **Gomu Gomu no Tonkachi** (Luffy) - Using objects as hammers
- **Colors Trap** (Miss Goldenweek) - Paint-based hypnosis
- **Uragiri no Kuro** - Makes target betray friends
- **Warai no Kiiro** - Induces uncontrollable laughter
- **Togyu no Aka** - Directs attacks like bullfighting
- **Kanashimi no Ao** - Makes target melancholic
- **Nagomi no Midori** - Causes extreme relaxation
- **Candle Wall** (Mr. 3) - Defensive wax barrier
- **Nose Fancy Double Cannon** (Mr. 5) - Dual explosive attack

## Themes
- Mental vs Physical Strength
- Courage in the Face of Fear
- Teamwork and Interdependence
- Intelligence over Brute Force

## Trivia
- Colors Trap is based on real color psychology theory
- NOT a Devil Fruit ability (confirmed in SBS Vol. 23)
- Chapter 124's title "This Tea is Really Delicious" references the Nagomi no Midori scene
- Simple-minded people like Luffy are more vulnerable to Colors Trap
- The anime changed a violent scene from the manga (helmet spike vs. wax dagger)

## Cultural Impact
- Introduces psychological warfare as a valid combat strategy in One Piece
- Shows that non-Devil Fruit abilities can be extremely powerful
- Foreshadows the importance of willpower in later arcs
- Miss Goldenweek becomes a fan-favorite underrated character

## Episode Significance
Episode 75 is crucial for:
1. Establishing that combat in One Piece isn't just about physical power
2. Demonstrating the diversity of Baroque Works' tactics
3. Developing Usopp as a braver warrior
4. Creating one of the most desperate situations for the crew in East Blue Saga
5. Introducing a unique ability system that doesn't rely on Devil Fruits
