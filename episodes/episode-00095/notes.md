# Research Notes for Episode 95

## Episode Overview
Episode 95 "Ace and Luffy! Hot Emotions and Brotherly Bonds!" is a pivotal episode in the Arabasta Arc that focuses on the relationship between Luffy and his older brother Ace. The episode aired on January 6, 2002 in Japan.

## Key Findings

### 1. First Debut of "Fish" Ending Theme
This episode marks the first time the ending theme "Fish" by The Kaleidoscope is used in the One Piece anime series. It would continue to be used through Episode 106.

### 2. Significant Anime-Original Content
Episode 95 contains substantial anime-original material that expands on the manga:
- The entire sequence where Luffy gets separated from the crew and fights Baroque Works Billions with Ace is anime-only
- The arm-wrestling scene is extended from the manga
- Ace's invitation to join Whitebeard Pirates is relocated to Nanohana (in the manga it happens on the Going Merry)
- The scene with Mr. Mellow and Geronimo killing Mr. 11 is entirely anime-original

### 3. Critical Foreshadowing
The episode contains extremely important foreshadowing for the Summit War saga:
- Ace's mention of hunting Blackbeard who killed Thatch
- This hunt will eventually lead to Ace's capture and execution
- Sets up one of the most emotionally devastating arcs in the series

### 4. First Full Display of Hiken
This is the first time we see Ace's signature technique "Hiken" (Fire Fist) in full action, destroying five ships in one attack. This establishes why he's called "Fire Fist Ace" and demonstrates the overwhelming power of a Whitebeard Pirates division commander.

### 5. Voice Actor Note
Ginzō Matsuo, who voiced Smoker, had already passed away on August 25, 2001, several months before this episode aired on January 6, 2002. Mahito Ōba took over the role starting with Episode 94.

## Notable Character Moments

### Ace
- Shows his protective nature toward Luffy
- Demonstrates incredible power gap between him and lower-level pirates
- Reveals his mission to hunt Blackbeard
- Maintains his cool, confident demeanor while being deadly serious about his mission

### Luffy
- Reaffirms his dream to become Pirate King independently
- Shows equal strength to Ace in arm-wrestling, demonstrating his growth
- Displays trust in his crew even when separated
- Values his independence over joining a more powerful crew

## Manga vs Anime Differences

### Major Changes
1. **Pacing**: Chapter 159 is split across multiple episodes (95, 96, and 101)
2. **Scene Order**: Ace's invitation moved from the Going Merry to Nanohana
3. **Added Content**: Billions attack sequence is entirely anime-original
4. **Expansion**: The arm-wrestling and bonding moments are extended

### Why These Changes Matter
The anime-original content serves several purposes:
- Extends the episode runtime to match pacing
- Gives more screen time to Ace before his eventual fate
- Demonstrates the Baroque Works organization's ruthlessness and hierarchy
- Provides more action sequences for the episode

## Thematic Elements

### Brotherhood
The episode emphasizes the strong bond between Ace and Luffy through:
- Playful competition (arm-wrestling)
- Fighting together as a team
- Mutual respect despite different paths
- Ace's protective instincts

### Dreams and Independence
Luffy's refusal to join Whitebeard Pirates reinforces:
- His unwavering determination to achieve his own dream
- The importance of forging his own path
- That he respects power but won't subordinate himself

### Power Hierarchy
The episode clearly establishes:
- Division between low-level pirates (Billions) and top-tier pirates (Ace)
- Why the Whitebeard Pirates are considered legendary
- The desperation of Baroque Works Billions to advance in rank
