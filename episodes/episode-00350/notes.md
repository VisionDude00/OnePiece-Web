# Research Notes for Episode 350

## Episode Information
- **Episode Number**: 350
- **Title**: "The Warrior Known as the Devil!! The Moment of Oars' Revival!"
- **Japanese Title**: 魔人と呼ばれた戦士！！オーズ復活の時 (Majin to Yobareta Senshi!! Ōzu Fukkatsu no Toki)
- **Air Date (Japan)**: April 20, 2008
- **Air Date (US)**: June 18, 2016
- **Manga Chapter**: Chapter 456 ("Demon from the Land of Ice")

## Research Summary

This episode marks a crucial turning point in the Thriller Bark arc with the introduction and resurrection of Oars, one of the most imposing antagonists in the series at this point. The episode also contains the first significant mention of Kaido, one of the Four Emperors, which would later become extremely important to the overall story.

## Key Highlights

### 1. Oars' Debut
- First appearance of Oars, the ancient giant standing 67 meters (219'10") tall and weighing 102 tons
- Oars died approximately 500 years ago in the Land of Ice from exposure/frostbite
- Known as "the Continent-Puller" who built a criminal nation
- His corpse was recovered by Moria and Hogback 12 years before the current events
- Hogback spent 10 years restoring and preserving the body

### 2. The Kaido Connection
- **CRITICAL FORESHADOWING**: This episode contains the first significant mention of Kaido in the series
- Moria reveals he was defeated by Kaido in the New World, which destroyed his original crew
- This defeat motivated Moria to create an invulnerable zombie army
- Moria's goal is revenge against Kaido and becoming Pirate King with his zombie forces
- This sets up Kaido as a major threat long before his actual appearance in the Wano arc

### 3. Brook's Character Development
- Brook demonstrates growth by choosing to confront Ryuma rather than running
- He shares crucial tactical information about zombies being weak to salt
- His explanation: shadows are taken via Devil Fruit powers, so seawater properties (salt) can purify zombies
- This shows Brook learned from his 5 years of experience after recovering his shadow initially

### 4. Shadow Personality Transfer
- The episode demonstrates that shadows carry the personality and memories of their original owners
- Oars immediately displays Luffy's personality upon awakening, shouting for "MEAT!"
- This creates both comedic moments and foreshadows control issues for Moria

## Interesting Observations

### 1. Long-term Planning
- The revelation that Hogback worked on Oars for 10 years shows how long Moria has been planning this
- This demonstrates the scale of Moria's ambition and the importance he places on this project
- Contrasts with Luffy's more spontaneous approach to achieving goals

### 2. Tim Burton Influence
- Multiple sources note that Thriller Bark arc is heavily influenced by Tim Burton's works
- Particularly "The Nightmare Before Christmas" which is one of Eiichiro Oda's favorite movies
- The episode's gothic horror atmosphere and zombie resurrection themes align with this influence

### 3. Opening Theme Significance
- "Jungle P" by 5050 was the first opening to feature both Franky and the Thousand Sunny
- Interestingly, Brook also appears in the opening even though he hadn't officially joined the crew yet at the start of the opening's run
- The "P" in "Jungle P" stands for "Party"

### 4. Kozuki Moria Theory Impact
- The Kozuki Moria theory (revealed in Chapter 1158's cover story) recontextualizes this entire episode
- If true, Moria's defeat by Kaido and desire for revenge takes on new meaning
- He wouldn't just be seeking power, but reclaiming what Kaido took from him (home, identity, honor)
- Episode 350's Kaido mention becomes even more significant as foreshadowing

## Fan Reception

### Positive Aspects
- Fans appreciated the escalation of threat with Oars' introduction
- The Kaido name-drop was recognized as significant even by viewers who hadn't read ahead
- Brook's character moments were well-received
- The episode effectively built tension for the arc's climax

### Mixed Reactions
- Some confusion about the "Kaido" name initially, with some viewers mishearing it as "Kitainu" or "Akainu"
- Pacing concerns typical of One Piece anime (some extended scenes)
- Oars' immediate personality (Luffy's) created both comedy and tension in ways that divided viewers

## Final Thoughts

Episode 350 successfully raises the stakes for the Thriller Bark arc while planting seeds for future storylines. The introduction of Oars creates an imposing physical threat, while the Kaido mention establishes a long-term narrative thread that won't be fully explored for hundreds of episodes. Brook's character development provides emotional weight, and the episode balances tension with comedy effectively through Oars' Luffy-influenced personality.

The episode represents One Piece's strength in long-term storytelling - events and mentions here have ramifications that extend far beyond the immediate arc, as evidenced by the Kozuki Moria theory based on information revealed over 800 chapters later.
