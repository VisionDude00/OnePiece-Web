# Episode 117 Research Notes

## Research Date
November 18, 2025

## Episode Overview
Episode 117 "Nami's Cyclone Advisory! Clima Takt Burst!" is a pivotal episode in the Arabasta Arc that serves two major narrative functions:
1. Nami's character development as a solo combatant
2. Revelation of Crocodile's true goal (acquiring Pluton)

## Key Observations

### Nami's Character Development
- This episode marks a turning point for Nami's character arc. Prior to this, she was primarily portrayed as a support character who avoided direct combat.
- The Clima-Tact's initial failure (party tricks instead of weapons) creates dramatic tension and humor while highlighting the miscommunication between Usopp and Nami about what she needed.
- Nami's ability to adapt the "useless" party trick weapon into an effective combat tool demonstrates her intelligence and meteorological expertise.
- The weapon is specifically designed to be useless to anyone without advanced weather knowledge, making it uniquely suited to Nami.

### Pluton Revelation
- Episode 117 contains the FIRST MENTION of Pluton in the One Piece anime, making it historically significant to the series' overarching plot.
- Crocodile's revelation that he wants Pluton to "counter the World Government" adds significant depth to his character - he's not just a power-hungry villain but someone with larger ambitions.
- The Pluton revelation connects to future arcs including Water 7/Enies Lobby (Franky's blueprints) and Wano (Pluton's location).

### Dual Narrative Structure
The episode expertly balances two parallel storylines:
1. Nami's comedic struggle with the Clima-Tact (lighter tone)
2. Crocodile's dark interrogation of Cobra (serious tone)

This creates tonal variety and maintains viewer engagement throughout the episode.

### Animation Notes
- Contains a notable animation error at 19:57 where Crocodile lights his cigar with both hands (hook disappears)
- Generally standard animation quality for the Arabasta arc
- The Clima-Tact techniques are well-animated, particularly the visual effects for Cyclone Tempo and Mirage Tempo

### Voice Acting
Japanese voice actors deliver strong performances:
- Akemi Okamura (Nami) effectively portrays both frustration with the weapon and growing confidence
- Ryuzaburo Otomo (Crocodile) delivers menacing dialogue with appropriate gravitas
- Misa Watanabe (Vivi) conveys despair convincingly

### Thematic Elements
**Intelligence vs. Strength**
- The episode emphasizes that combat effectiveness comes from knowledge and creativity, not just raw power
- Nami defeats a Devil Fruit user without having a Devil Fruit herself

**Knowledge as Power**
- Both storylines feature knowledge as the central power: Nami's meteorology and the ancient knowledge of Pluton
- The Poneglyph that reveals Pluton's location represents forbidden knowledge

**Desperation and Hope**
- Vivi experiences crushing despair (Luffy's death, the bomb)
- Nami finds hope through understanding her weapon
- Creates emotional contrast between the two female leads
