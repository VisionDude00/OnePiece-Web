# Research Notes - Episode 136

## Episode Overview
Episode 136 "Zenny of the Island of Goats and the Pirate Ship in the Mountains!" is the first episode of the Goat Island Arc, a three-episode filler arc (episodes 136-138).

## Research Process

### Sources Used
- One Piece Wiki (Fandom) - Primary source for episode details, character information, and production credits
- IMDb - Voice actor information and episode ratings
- Behind The Voice Actors - Additional voice actor details
- Multiple anime blogs and articles for reviews and fan perspectives

### Key Findings

#### Episode Details
- **Air Date (Japan):** December 8, 2002
- **Air Date (US):** May 11, 2010
- **Opening:** "Hikari e" by The Babystars (episodes 116-168)
- **Ending:** "Free Will" by Ruppina (episodes 133-156)
- **Director:** Junichi Fujise
- **Animation Director:** Kazuya Hisada
- **Writer:** Yoshiyuki Suga
- **Storyboard:** Kenji Yokoyama

#### Character Debuts
1. **Zenny** - Elderly man living on Goat Island, former money lender, diagnosed with terminal heart condition
   - Japanese VA: Osamu Saka
   - English VA: Charles Campbell

2. **Minchey** - Marine Chief Petty Officer, arc antagonist
   - Japanese VA: Takuma Suzuki
   - English VA: Jerry Jewell

3. **Captain Moore** - Marine Captain, Minchey's superior
   - Japanese VA: Ryoichi Tanaka
   - English VA: Not found

#### Location Debuts
- **Goat Island** - Uncharted island inhabited by Zenny and numerous goats, features mysterious pirate ship in mountains

### Filler Context
This is a non-canon filler arc that occurs between:
- **Before:** Post-Alabasta filler episodes (131-135)
- **After:** Rainbow Mist filler arc (139-143)
- **In manga timeline:** This would fall between Alabasta Arc and Jaya/Skypiea Arc

The arc was skipped entirely in the 4Kids English dub.

### Reception
Mixed reviews from the community:
- **Positive:** Fun three-episode diversion, engaging character in Zenny, good themes about dreams and mortality
- **Negative:** Considered easily skippable, some find it drags, inconsequential to main story
- **Rating:** Poggers gave it 7.8/10 for engaging storytelling
