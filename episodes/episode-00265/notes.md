# Research Notes for Episode 265

## Episode Overview
Episode 265 "Luffy Cuts Through! Big Showdown on the Judicial Island!" marks the beginning of the assault on Enies Lobby's main gate. This episode is significant as it introduces two major characters (Kashii and Oimo) and showcases the unity of the allied forces.

## Research Process

### Sources Consulted
- One Piece Wiki (Fandom) - Primary source for episode details, character information, and manga chapter references
- Behind The Voice Actors - Voice actor credits for both Japanese and English versions
- Tumblr blog "Never Watched One Piece" - Episode review with detailed observations
- Various Bilibili reaction videos - Community reception and fan engagement
- News articles about the giant characters

### Key Findings

#### Episode Details
- Air Date: June 4, 2006 (Japan), June 29, 2013 (US Funimation)
- Adapts manga chapters 376-378
- Opening: "Brand New World" by D-51 (episodes 264-278)
- Ending: "Adventure World" by Delicatessen (episodes 264-278)
- Director: Hiroyuki Kakudō
- Writer: Hirohiko Kamisaka

#### Character Debuts
- **Kashii**: Giant gatekeeper, 1700 cm tall, voiced by Kōhei Fukuhara (JP) and Jeff Johnson (EN)
- **Oimo**: Giant gatekeeper, 1600 cm tall, voiced by Hiroshi Okamoto (JP) and Tyson Rinehart (EN)

Both giants are former members of the Giant Warrior Pirates who served under Dorry and Brogy. They were deceived by the World Government into believing their captains were imprisoned, leading them to serve as gatekeepers for 50 years.

#### New Techniques
- **Marine Puppet Technique** (Luffy): Creative use of Gomu Gomu powers to control an enemy Marine
- **Rope Action: Figure of Eight Knot** (Paulie): Binding technique using shipwright expertise

#### Key Story Elements
1. Luffy's solo assault demonstrates his determination and growing tactical creativity
2. The Franky Family and Galley-La Company unite despite past conflicts
3. The World Government's manipulation of Kashii and Oimo reveals their ruthless methods
4. Teamwork overcomes individual power (defeating Kashii through combined effort)

### Observations

#### Thematic Elements
- **Deception vs. Truth**: The giants' storyline emphasizes the World Government's willingness to manipulate loyal individuals
- **Unity in Diversity**: Former enemies working together demonstrates the power of common cause
- **David vs. Goliath**: Smaller forces using strategy to overcome larger opponents

#### Animation Quality
According to the blog review, the episode featured impressive slow-motion action sequences and lighter comedic moments, particularly with Luffy's Marine puppet technique.

#### Cultural References
- Kashii and Oimo's names reference the biblical cities Sodom and Gomorrah
- Sodom and Gomorrah (the King Bulls) share this naming theme
- The connection to judgment/destruction fits the setting of the Judicial Island
