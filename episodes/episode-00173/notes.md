# Research Notes for Episode 173

## Episode Overview
Episode 173 "The Invincible Ability! Enel's True Nature Revealed" is a pivotal episode in the Skypiea Arc that establishes Enel as one of the most formidable antagonists in One Piece through the revelation of his Goro Goro no Mi Devil Fruit powers.

## Key Research Findings

### Primary Sources
- Episode corresponds primarily to **Chapter 264** ("Warrior Kamakiri vs. God Enel")
- Air Date: November 9, 2003 (Japan), November 6, 2008 (US)
- Part of the Skypiea Arc (Episodes 153-195)

### Major Events Documented
1. **Enel's Five-Minute Challenge** - One of the most memorable scenes showing Enel's god complex
2. **Logia Intangibility Demonstration** - Clear visual of Kamakiri's spear passing through Enel's head
3. **1,000,000 Volt Vari** - First named voltage attack, defeats 21 fighters simultaneously
4. **Nami's Combat Growth** - First use of Impact Dial, demonstrates strategic fighting
5. **Survival Game Progress** - Drops from 81 to 25 participants

### Character Analysis

#### Enel
This episode is crucial for Enel's character establishment. The five-minute challenge perfectly encapsulates his psychology:
- Supreme confidence bordering on absolute certainty
- Views mortals as beneath his concern
- Demonstrates his "godhood" through overwhelming power
- Shows both his arrogance and his justification for it (he genuinely IS nearly invincible)

#### Kamakiri
- Represents the "strength isn't enough" theme
- Despite being one of the strongest Shandia warriors, he's completely outmatched
- His defeat emphasizes the hopelessness of the situation
- His bravery in accepting the challenge shows Shandia determination

#### Nami
- Significant character development in combat capabilities
- Shows strategic thinking over raw power
- First major use of dial technology in battle
- Demonstrates she can hold her own without Luffy/Zoro/Sanji

### Themes Identified
1. **Powerlessness vs. Overwhelming Force** - Central to the episode
2. **Strategic vs. Raw Power** - Nami's victories contrast with Kamakiri's defeat
3. **God Complex and Hubris** - Enel's characterization
4. **Logical Weaknesses** - Foreshadowing Luffy's rubber immunity

### Notable Details
- The conductive nature of Milky Road amplifies Enel's attack range
- Enel's voltage attacks are numbered (1M, 30M, 60M, 100M, 200M volts in later episodes)
- This is the first time the Goro Goro no Mi is explicitly named
- The episode showcases why Logia fruits are considered "invincible"

## Final Notes
This episode successfully transforms Enel from a threatening presence to a seemingly insurmountable obstacle. The five-minute challenge scene is masterfully executed to build tension while showcasing Enel's character. The parallel storylines (Enel's dominance vs. Nami's strategic victory) create good narrative balance.

The Goro Goro no Mi revelation is handled as a major story beat, emphasizing its importance to the arc and to the broader One Piece world. This episode serves as the true beginning of Enel as the arc's final boss.
