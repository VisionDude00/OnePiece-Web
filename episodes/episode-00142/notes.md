## Continuity Notes

### Important Continuity Issue
The arc creates a continuity problem with later canon content:
- The Straw Hats encounter time displacement in this filler arc
- In the Wano Country Arc (much later), characters express surprise at time travel
- This suggests either:
  1. The crew forgot about the Rainbow Mist
  2. Oda didn't consider filler when writing Wano
  3. The filler isn't considered part of the Straw Hats' "real" experiences

### 4Kids Dub Note
- This was the **last arc dubbed by 4Kids Entertainment** before they lost the One Piece license
- 4Kids aired it on September 15, 2007
- 4Kids renamed "Ape's Concert" to "Wrecker's Reef"
- Lake was given an Australian accent in the 4Kids version

## Interesting Trivia

1. **Name Pun**: "Ape's Concert" is a pun - the Japanese pronunciation of "Sargasso" (サルガッソ) sounds like "saru gassō" (猿合奏), meaning "monkey/ape concert"

2. **Going Merry Damage**: The arc explains why the Going Merry needs repairs - it sustains significant damage when expelled from Ape's Concert, losing its figurehead and a mast

3. **Time Mechanics**: The Rainbow Mist creates extreme time dilation - the Pumpkin Pirates remained physically as children for 50 years

4. **Generational Villainy**: Wetton's influence spans three generations (himself, son Flip, grandson Lake)
