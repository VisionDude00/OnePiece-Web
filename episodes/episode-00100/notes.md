# Episode 100 Research Notes

## Overview
Episode 100 is a milestone episode that focuses on emotional backstory rather than action. It's a flashback-heavy episode that establishes crucial character relationships and motivations for the Alabasta arc.

## Key Research Findings

### Episode Significance
- This is Episode 100, making it a significant milestone
- The episode appropriately focuses on deep character development and emotional storytelling
- Primarily flashback-based, showing events from 11 years before the main story
- Sets up the tragic irony of the Alabasta conflict

### Character Debuts
**First Appearances in Episode 100:**
- Toto (Koza's father) - though Koza himself first appeared in Episode 93
- Agotogi (kidnapper/bandit)
- Sand-Sand Clan members: Okame, Kebi, Erik, Natto, Aswa (in flashback)

**Note:** Koza appears extensively in flashback but technically debuted in Episode 93. Episode 100 provides his crucial backstory.

### Location Debuts
- Yuba - First mentioned and established as the destination for Toto and Koza
- More detailed look at Alubarna Palace (though it appeared earlier)

### Timeline
- Flashback takes place approximately 11 years before the current story
- Shows Vivi and Koza as children
- Koza receives his scar as a child, explaining his adult appearance

### Story Structure
The episode uses a frame narrative:
- Present: Straw Hats traveling through desert with Vivi and Ace
- Vivi tells Nami her story
- Extended flashback to childhood
- Return to present with revelation that Koza leads the rebellion

### Emotional Themes
1. **Friendship across class lines** - Princess befriends commoner children
2. **Sacrifice and nobility** - Koza's willingness to be wounded protecting Vivi
3. **Tragedy of manipulation** - Friends becoming enemies through Crocodile's schemes
4. **Loss of innocence** - Childhood happiness contrasted with present conflict
