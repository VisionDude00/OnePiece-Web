# Episode 70 Research Notes

## Episode Overview
**Title:** An Ancient Island! The Shadow Hiding in Little Garden!
**Air Date:** May 27, 2001 (Japan)
**Arc:** Little Garden Arc (First Episode)

## Key Research Findings

### Character Debuts
- **Mr. 3 (Galdino)**: Baroque Works Officer Agent with Doru Doru no Mi
- **Miss Goldenweek (Marianne)**: Mr. 3's partner with Colors Trap ability
- **Samurai Batts**: Mr. 3's transponder snail
- **Brogy**: Appears only as shadow/silhouette at episode's end (full debut Episode 71)

### Location Debuts
- **Little Garden**: Prehistoric island frozen in time with dinosaurs
- **Kyuka Island**: Baroque Works vacation resort

### Devil Fruit Debut
- **Doru Doru no Mi (Wax-Wax Fruit)**: First demonstrated by Mr. 3

### Important Story Elements

1. **Zoro-Sanji Rivalry Origin**: This episode formally establishes their competitive dynamic through the hunting competition

2. **Voice Actor Change**: Wakana Yamazaki begins substituting for Akemi Okamura as Nami's voice (episodes 70-78) due to maternity leave

3. **Manga Coverage**: Episode adapts portions of chapters 115 and 117, notably skipping chapter 116 entirely

4. **Log Pose Setting**: Little Garden requires 1 year for Log Pose to set - longest shown at this point in series

5. **Foreshadowing**: Introduction of giants and Elbaf culture sets up storylines that pay off hundreds of episodes later

## Episode Events Breakdown

### Act 1: Baroque Works (0:00-5:30)
- Mr. 3 and Miss Goldenweek on Kyuka Island
- Wax house demonstration
- Orders received about Straw Hats

### Act 2: Arrival at Little Garden (5:30-11:00)
- Going Merry approaches island
- Nami recalls Louis Arnote's account
- Crew decides to split up

### Act 3: Exploration (11:00-20:00)
- Zoro-Sanji hunting competition begins
- Luffy and Vivi encounter Brachiosaurus
- Sanji battles T-Rex
- Zoro encounters Triceratops
- Nami and Usopp guard ship

### Act 4: Cliffhanger (20:00-22:00)
- Giant's shadow appears in jungle
- Sets up Brogy's full introduction in Episode 71

## Trivia & Easter Eggs

1. **Pandaman Appearance**: Continues series' longest-running Easter egg
2. **Mr. 3's Hair**: Changes shape based on emotions (forms punctuation marks)
3. **Episode 70 Significance**: Exactly at the beginning of the Little Garden arc, which is one of the shorter arcs (only 8 episodes)

## Thematic Elements

### Primary Themes
1. **Adventure and Exploration**: Excitement and danger of Grand Line exploration
2. **Rivalry and Competition**: Zoro-Sanji dynamic established
3. **Courage vs Caution**: Contrasting approaches to danger

### Symbolism
- Little Garden represents Grand Line's unpredictable nature
- Prehistoric setting evokes classic adventure stories
- Giants parallel the Zoro-Sanji rivalry (honorable combat between equals)

## Anime vs Manga Differences

1. Extended scenes of Nami barefoot on ship (anime-original)
2. Additional Usopp-Nami comedy interactions for pacing
3. Expanded wax house demonstration scene
4. Chapter 116 skipped in adaptation

## Long-term Significance

This episode is particularly important for:

1. **Elbaf Foreshadowing**: Sets up giant culture for future arcs
2. **Character Dynamics**: Establishes Zoro-Sanji rivalry pattern
3. **Baroque Works**: Introduces more sophisticated agents
4. **World-Building**: Shows Grand Line's temporal anomalies
