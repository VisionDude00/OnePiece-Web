# Episode 548: The Kingdom in Shock! An Order to Execute Neptune Issued!

**Air Date:** May 20, 2012
**Arc:** Fish-Man Island Arc
**Saga:** Fish-Man Island Saga

## Quick Summary

Hody Jones broadcasts his coup d'Ã©tat across all of Fish-Man Island, announcing King Neptune's execution in three hours and declaring himself the new ruler. Meanwhile, Nico Robin makes a groundbreaking discovery - a Poneglyph in the Sea Forest containing an apology from Joy Boy, marking the first mention of this mysterious figure in the anime. Luffy and Jinbe clash over whether Luffy should fight Hody, with Jinbe fearing human involvement will worsen Fish-Man/human relations.

## Major Events

1. **Joy Boy's First Mention** - Robin discovers Joy Boy's apology on the Poneglyph
2. **Hody's Coup Broadcast** - Announces Neptune's execution and his new rule
3. **Otohime Memorial Desecration** - Citizens forced to step on Queen Otohime's portrait
4. **Straw Hats Threatened** - Hody announces plans to execute captured crew members
5. **Luffy vs. Jinbe Disagreement** - Conflict begins over who should fight Hody

## Key Characters

- **Nico Robin** - Discovers Joy Boy's apology

- **Monkey D. Luffy** - Determined to save his crew despite consequences
- **Jinbe** - Opposes Luffy's intervention for political reasons
- **King Neptune** - Held captive, scheduled for execution

## Significance

This episode is historically important as it introduces **Joy Boy** to the One Piece anime, a character who becomes crucial to understanding the series' ultimate mysteries. The episode also deepens the Fish-Man Island conflict while exploring complex themes of racism, persecution, and the tension between personal loyalty and political responsibility.

## Cultural Notes

The scene where citizens are forced to step on Queen Otohime's portrait references the Japanese historical practice of "fumi-e" - the persecution of Christians during the Edo period who were forced to step on images of Jesus or Mary.

## Themes

- Legacy and Inherited Will
- Loyalty vs. Greater Good
- Persecution and Ideological Suppression
