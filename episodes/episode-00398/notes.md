# Research Notes for Episode 398

## Episode Summary
**Title:** "Admiral Kizaru Takes Action! Sabaody Archipelago Thrown into Chaos"
**Air Date:** April 26, 2009 (Japan)
**Arc:** Sabaody Archipelago Arc
**Manga Chapter:** 504

## Key Research Findings

### 1. Major Milestone Episode
Episode 398 is historically significant for several reasons:
- **First explicit mention of "Haki" by name** in the anime (spoken by Hatchan)
- **First demonstration of Conqueror's Haki** in the anime (by Rayleigh)
- **First display of Law's Ope Ope no Mi** abilities
- **First display of Kid's Jiki Jiki no Mi** abilities
- **First mention of "SMILEs"** - remarkable foreshadowing that doesn't pay off for 200+ episodes

### 2. SMILE Foreshadowing
The SMILE mention is one of the most impressive pieces of long-term planning in One Piece:
- Mentioned in Episode 398 (April 2009)
- Doesn't appear or get mentioned again until Episode 615 (May 2013)
- Gap of **217 episodes / 4 years**
- Becomes central to Punk Hazard, Dressrosa, and Wano arcs

### 3. Haki Introduction Strategy
The episode strategically introduces Haki in multiple ways:
1. Visual demonstration (Conqueror's Haki knockout)
2. Practical application (removing explosive collar)
3. Explicit naming (Hatchan explains)
4. Different types shown (Conqueror's and Advanced Armament)

This multi-layered introduction ensures viewers understand Haki is a significant new power system.

### 4. Character Debut
Admiral Kizaru (Borsalino) makes his debut in this episode, though only in scenes at Marine Headquarters. He doesn't actually arrive at Sabaody until the next episode.

### 5. The Supernova Trio
The iconic visual of Luffy, Law, and Kid standing together outside the auction house:
- Creates one of the most memorable images of the Sabaody arc
- Foreshadows their future alliance in Wano
- Shows three different Devil Fruit powers (Paramecia types with very different applications)
- Establishes the competitive dynamic between Luffy and Kid

### 6. Voice Acting Note
Unshō Ishizuka voices Kizaru in this episode. He passed away in August 2018 and was replaced by Ryōtarō Okiayu. This is important for historical accuracy.

### 7. Opening/Ending Songs
- **Opening:** "Share the World" by Tohoshinki (TVXQ) - Episodes 395-425
- **Ending:** None (the series had stopped using regular ending themes at this point)

### 8. Animation Quality
The episode is noted for having HIGH animation quality, particularly during:
- Rayleigh's Haki demonstration scene
- The three Supernovas displaying their Devil Fruit powers
- Visual effects for Law's "Room" ability

## Notable Observations

### 1. Episode Rating
This episode has a 9.0/10 rating on IMDB, making it one of the highest-rated episodes of the Sabaody Archipelago arc. This reflects fan appreciation for:
- The introduction of Haki
- The Supernova team-up moment
- Rayleigh's impressive power display
- The escalating tension with Kizaru's dispatch

### 2. Thematic Importance
The episode embodies the theme of "generational change":
- Old generation (Rayleigh) demonstrates power
- New generation (Luffy, Kid, Law) takes the lead
- Rayleigh trusts the rookies to handle their own escape
- Symbolic passing of the torch

### 3. Story Architecture
Episode 398 serves as a perfect "calm before the storm":
- Resolves immediate tension (collar removal, slaves freed)
- Establishes new power systems (Haki)
- Sets up future catastrophe (Kizaru dispatched)
- Plants long-term story seeds (SMILEs)
