# Research Notes for Episode 359

## Episode Overview
**Title:** "A Clear-Clear History? Sanji's Stolen Dream"
**Air Date (Japan):** June 22, 2008
**Air Date (US):** August 27, 2016
**Arc:** Thriller Bark
**Manga Chapters:** 463-464

## Key Research Findings

### 1. Censorship and Historical Context

This episode is historically significant due to its censorship in the Japanese broadcast. The episode aired just two weeks after the Akihabara massacre (June 8, 2008), where a man stabbed multiple people with a knife, killing several. Out of sensitivity to this tragedy:

- The TV broadcast version removed stabbing scenes where Absalom stabs Sanji
- These scenes were replaced with kicks instead
- Interestingly, Sanji's dialogue about "getting blood on Nami's dress" was retained even in the censored version
- The DVD release and international versions restored the original stabbing scenes
- The English dub uses the altered sound effects from the TV version

This represents a rare case of real-world events directly affecting One Piece's anime production and is worth noting for historical/production research.

### 2. Sanji's Character Development

Episode 359 provides crucial character development for Sanji:

**Surface Reading:**
- Sanji reveals he wanted the Suke Suke no Mi to become invisible and "peep on women"
- This seems like pure comedy/fan service

**Deeper Analysis:**
- Fan theories suggest a darker interpretation: as a child being abused by his Vinsmoke family, Sanji may have wanted invisibility to escape/hide from them
- This adds tragic depth to what appears to be a comedic moment
- The episode shows Sanji had knowledge of Devil Fruits from childhood (reading the encyclopedia)
- This is the first time we learn Sanji considered eating a Devil Fruit despite the swimming curse

**Combat Showcase:**
- Sanji demonstrates overwhelming power against Absalom when motivated by rage
- Shows his protective nature toward Nami and women in general
- His knowledge of how Devil Fruits work (explaining Absalom's power mechanics)

### 3. Three Concurrent Battles Structure

The episode masterfully juggles three separate battle threads:

1. **Sanji vs Absalom** (Ceremony Hall)
   - Character-driven fight with emotional stakes
   - Reveals backstory and motivations
   - Physical combat with clear advantage to Sanji

2. **Luffy vs Moria/Doppelman** (Mast Mansion)
   - Strategic puzzle-solving combat
   - Luffy needs to figure out how to bypass the shadow
   - First successful hit using Gomu Gomu no Stamp

3. **Usopp vs Perona** (Mansion)
   - Setup for future payoff
   - Chase sequence leading to confrontation
   - Introduction of Ghost Princess persona
   - Sets up the famous "negative vs negative" battle

### 4. Devil Fruit Mechanics Revealed

**Suke Suke no Mi (Clear-Clear Fruit):**
- Can make the user invisible
- Can also make other objects/people invisible by touching them
- Doesn't prevent sounds or physical traces (footprints, blood)
- Sanji demonstrates tactical knowledge of its limitations

**Horo Horo no Mi (Hollow-Hollow Fruit):**
- Astral projection ability revealed for first time
- Perona can leave her physical body as an intangible ghost
- Sets up importance for future battle (body vs projection vulnerability)

**Kage Kage no Mi (Shadow-Shadow Fruit):**
- Doppelman can transform into Brick Bats (bat-shaped shadows)
- Shows versatility of Moria's shadow manipulation
- Reveals that Moria's real body is still vulnerable even when using Doppelman

### 5. Foreshadowing and Setup

**Immediate Setup (Episodes 360-361):**
- Sanji's battle with Absalom continues
- Usopp vs Perona battle begins
- Luffy continues finding ways to attack Moria

**Long-term Significance:**
- The Suke Suke no Mi later goes to Shiryu of Blackbeard Pirates (post-time skip)
- Fan theories about the fruit being more special than it appears (Blackbeard wanted it)
- Usopp vs Perona becomes one of the most memorable battles showcasing character matchups

### 7. Character Moments Worth Noting

**Nami:**
- Remains unconscious throughout, but her presence motivates Sanji
- Symbolizes what Sanji is fighting to protect

**Kumashi (Zombie Bear):**
- Defeated by Usopp using fire attacks
- Shows effectiveness of fire against zombies
- Comic relief amid serious battles

**Perona's "Ghost Princess" Declaration:**
- Theatrical self-introduction
- Establishes her as a dramatic, prideful character
- Floating outside window in astral form creates memorable visual

### 8. Thematic Elements

**Dreams and Desires:**
- Both Sanji and Luffy are pursuing their goals with determination
- Sanji's "stolen dream" creates personal stakes in his fight
- Shows how even childhood wishes can fuel present actions

**Creativity in Battle:**
- Luffy's unconventional attack angle (swinging from ledge)
- Usopp's use of fire and oil against Kumashi
- Strategic thinking over raw power

**Protection and Chivalry:**
- Sanji's primary motivation is protecting Nami
- His rage amplified by Absalom's actions toward her
- Traditional chivalric code driving his fighting spirit

### 9. Fan Reception and Impact

**Positive Reception:**
- Sanji's backstory reveal is memorable
- The setup for Usopp vs Perona is well-regarded
- Good balance of comedy and action

**Criticisms:**
- Some fans note Sanji doesn't finish Absalom himself (Nami delivers final blow in later episode)
- Perverted humor may not appeal to all viewers
- Some feel individual Thriller Bark fights aren't as strong as other arcs

**Iconic Moments:**
- Sanji's "stolen dream" speech
- Perona's Ghost Princess reveal
- Luffy's creative Gomu Gomu no Stamp

### 10. Cultural and Historical Context

**Japanese Broadcast Timing:**
- Episode 359 aired during Season 10 of One Piece
- The series was already 359 episodes in, showing its longevity
- Thriller Bark arc was polarizing among fans (some love it, some find it weaker)
- The Akihabara incident caused significant changes to Japanese media portrayal of violence

**Western Release:**
- 8+ year gap between Japanese and US broadcast
- Funimation handled the English dub
- Released during One Piece's growing Western popularity

### 14. Personal Observations

This episode is a great example of One Piece's ability to blend:
- Comedy (Sanji's perverted motivation)
- Tragedy (potential darker reading of Sanji's childhood)
- Action (three concurrent battles)
- Strategy (creative combat solutions)
- Character development (backstory reveals)
- World-building (Devil Fruit mechanics)

The censorship context makes it historically significant beyond just the story content. It shows how anime production can be affected by real-world events and cultural sensitivity.

The episode serves primarily as a "middle chapter" in larger battle sequences, setting up payoffs rather than delivering them. This is typical of One Piece's pacing during the Thriller Bark arc.
