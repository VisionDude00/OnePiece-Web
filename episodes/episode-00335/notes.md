# Research Notes - Episode 335

## Episode Overview
- **Title:** "Waiting in the New World! Farewell to the Courageous Pirates"
- **Air Date:** December 16, 2007
- **Arc:** Ice Hunter Arc (Final Episode - Episodes 326-335)
- **Type:** Anime-only filler arc

## Key Research Findings

### Episode Content
This episode serves as the finale for the Ice Hunter Arc, a 10-episode anime-only filler arc that takes place between the Water 7 Saga and the Thriller Bark Arc.

**Main Plot Points:**
1. Luffy defeats Don Accino using the anime-exclusive technique "Gomu Gomu no Hyakuman-do Bazooka"
2. The Straw Hats recover their stolen Jolly Roger flag with help from Lil
3. Lovely Land (Accino Family headquarters) is destroyed
4. Arbell and Lil reconcile their sisterly relationship
5. The Phoenix Pirates battle the remaining Accino Family members
6. Puzzle promises to meet the Straw Hats again in the New World

### Notable Anime-Exclusive Elements

**Gomu Gomu no Hyakuman-do Bazooka:**
- This technique is completely anime-original and never appears in the manga
- The name translates to "1 Million Degree Bazooka"
- It references Don Accino's 10,000-degree heat, with Luffy claiming his spirit burns 100 times hotter
- Luffy pushes through extreme pain as his hands are burned by Don Accino's heat

**Atsu Atsu no Mi:**
- Don Accino's Devil Fruit is anime-only
- Allows him to heat his body up to 10,000 degrees Celsius
- Later receives a canonical counterpart: Charlotte Oven's Netsu Netsu no Mi in the Whole Cake Island Arc
- Shows multiple heat-based techniques including Net Tire and Steam Iron 10,000 Degree Press

**Combination Play:**
- The magnetic ability used by Campacino and Brindo
- NOT a Devil Fruit power, but a natural ability the twins possess
- Allows them to attract or repel each other by glowing different colors

### Reception and Reviews

**Positive:**
- The Adult Swim Squad gave Episode 335 a 4.5/5 rating
- Praised as "a great way to close out the Acchino family arc"
- Appreciated the final battle between Luffy and Don Accino
- Noted that One Piece filler is "pretty consistent" compared to other anime

**Negative:**
- Poggers review gave the Ice Hunter Arc 2/10 for characterization
- Criticized for being too long (10 episodes) with minimal character development
- GameRant noted it would "fare much better" if it was half as long
- Described as "a slog to get through" by some reviewers

### Production Details

**Opening Theme:**
- "Jungle P" by 5050 (FIFTY-FIFTY)
- Used for episodes 326-372
- Released November 7, 2007

**No Ending Theme:**
- Starting from Episode 279, Toei Animation stopped airing ending themes
- Opening themes were extended from 110 seconds to 150 seconds instead
- This practice continued through Episode 335 and beyond

**Credits:**
- Writer: Koga Naoki
- Director: Hiroaki Miyamoto
- Animation Director: Kenji Yokoyama
- Art Director: Miyuki Sato, Shouji Tokiwa

**Voice Actors:**
- Don Accino (JP): Tomomichi Nishimura
- Don Accino (EN): Ray Gestaut

### Character Relationships

**Arbell & Lil:**
- The reconciliation between these sisters provides emotional closure to the arc
- Lil felt neglected when Arbell married Salchow
- In Episode 335, Arbell apologizes and promises to spend more time with Lil
- They dance together at the end, symbolizing their healed relationship

**Phoenix Pirates:**
- Led by Captain Puzzle (100,000,000 berry bounty)
- Lost their vice-captain Vigaro in the New World
- Had their Jolly Roger stolen by the Accino Family
- Promise to reunite with the Straw Hats in the New World

## Final Observations

Episode 335 serves as a satisfying conclusion to the Ice Hunter Arc despite mixed reception. While the arc received criticism for its length, this finale episode specifically was praised for its action and emotional moments. The reconciliation between Arbell and Lil, combined with Puzzle's promise to meet again in the New World, provides both emotional closure and forward momentum.

As a filler episode, it's notable for introducing the anime-exclusive Gomu Gomu no Hyakuman-do Bazooka technique and showcasing the full extent of the Atsu Atsu no Mi's powers. The destruction of Lovely Land serves as a clear symbolic ending to this chapter of the journey.

The episode demonstrates One Piece's commitment to quality even in filler content, maintaining character consistency and providing entertaining battles while avoiding long-term story implications.
