## Key Findings

### Episode Significance
This episode concludes the three-episode Goat Island Arc, a filler arc that doesn't appear in the manga. Despite being filler, the episode reinforces several core One Piece themes:
- Dreams have no age limit
- True treasure is companionship, not material wealth
- Honor vs. corruption within organizations

### Character Analysis

**Zenny**
- Main character of the arc
- Elderly man with heart condition who refuses to give up on his dream of becoming a pirate
- His character arc embodies the series' message about pursuing dreams regardless of circumstances
- Reveals that his "treasure" (goats who ate his money) is more valuable than gold

**Minchey**
- Corrupt Marine officer serving as the antagonist
- Represents the flawed side of the Marine organization
- Defeated and stripped of rank by his superior, Captain Moore

**Captain Moore**
- Honorable Marine captain
- Provides balance by showing that not all Marines are corrupt
- Shows mercy to the Straw Hats after they help expose Minchey's corruption

### Thematic Elements
1. **Age is No Barrier to Dreams** - Zenny's determination despite being elderly and sick
2. **Material vs. Spiritual Wealth** - The goats represent true treasure (companionship) vs. money
3. **Justice Within Systems** - Moore represents honorable authority confronting corruption
4. **Inspiration** - How the Straw Hats inspire others to chase their dreams
