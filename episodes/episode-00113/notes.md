# Research Notes for Episode 113

## Episode: "Alubarna is Crying! Fierce Fight of Captain Karoo!"

### Research Date
November 18, 2025

### Research Summary
Episode 113 is a pivotal episode in the Arabasta Arc that sets up multiple significant battles and showcases character development for several characters, particularly Karoo and the beginning of Usopp's heroic arc.

### Key Findings

#### Devil Fruit Debuts
- **Mogu Mogu no Mi (Mole-Mole Fruit)**: Miss Merry Christmas's Devil Fruit is revealed, allowing her to transform into a mole and create underground tunnel networks.
- **Inu Inu no Mi, Model: Dachshund**: Lassoo is revealed as the FIRST inanimate object shown to have consumed a Devil Fruit, which is a significant world-building moment.

#### Character Spotlights
1. **Karoo**: This episode is arguably Karoo's finest moment. He demonstrates:
   - Intelligence by recognizing Mr. 2's disguise
   - Unwavering loyalty to Vivi
   - Heroic determination inspired by Luffy's friendship
   - Willingness to sacrifice himself despite severe injuries

2. **Usopp & Chopper**: Sets up their character development battle against overwhelming odds

3. **Sanji**: Demonstrates his chivalrous nature fighting through both armies

#### Notable Elements

**Opening Song**: "Believe" by Folder 5 (Episodes 48-115)
- This is one of the last episodes to use this beloved opening theme

**Ending Song**: "GLORY -Kimi ga Iru Kara-" by Takako Uehara (Episodes 107-118)
- Features Vivi prominently, fitting for this Arabasta-focused episode

**Animation Quality**: Standard for the era (2002), animated by Kazuya Hisada

### Important Story Elements

**Foreshadowing**:
- Lassoo eating a Devil Fruit through "special technology" is the first mention of Vegapunk's ability to feed Devil Fruits to inanimate objects, which becomes more important much later in the series.

**Themes**:
- Friendship inspiring courage (Karoo remembering Luffy's words)
- Courage despite fear (Usopp and Chopper facing impossible odds)
- The tragedy of war affecting innocents (Karoo shot by stray bullet)
