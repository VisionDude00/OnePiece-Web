# Research Notes for Episode 120

## Research Date
November 18, 2025

## Episode Title
**English:** "The Battle is Over! Koza Raises the White Flag!"
**Japanese:** 戦いは終わった！コーザが掲げた白い旗 (Tatakai wa owatta! Kōza ga ageta shiroi hata)

## Key Observations

### Episode Structure
- This episode covers manga chapters 196-197
- The episode balances tragic sacrifice (Tsumegeri Guards) with political intrigue (Koza's peace attempt)
- Strong focus on Crocodile's overwhelming power and the futility of fighting without proper knowledge/preparation

### Significant Moments

1. **Tsumegeri Guards Sacrifice**
   - First and only appearance of these four guards (Hyota, Brahm, Arrow, Barrel)
   - Hero Water is a unique Alabasta-specific power enhancement
   - Their deaths are particularly tragic as Crocodile doesn't even dignify them with a real fight
   - Demonstrates the desperation of Alabasta's defenders

2. **Chaka's Devil Fruit Reveal**
   - First on-screen demonstration of the Inu Inu no Mi, Model: Jackal
   - Establishes Chaka as one of Alabasta's two guardian deities (Anubis)
   - His defeat further emphasizes Crocodile's superiority
   - Howling Fangs technique shown but ineffective

3. **The White Flag Scene**
   - Central to the episode's title and emotional core
   - Shows Koza's immediate willingness to end the war upon learning the truth
   - The shooting is a pivotal moment - Baroque Works' final sabotage
   - Demonstrates how thoroughly Crocodile has planned for every contingency

4. **Bomb Timer**
   - Creates urgent tension with 25 minutes remaining
   - Five-kilometer blast radius would destroy everyone
   - Hidden in the clock tower, guarded by Mr. 7 and Miss Father's Day

### Character Development

**Koza:**
- Shows his true character - immediately tries to stop the war when he learns the truth
- His shooting is particularly tragic as he's literally shot in the back while proclaiming peace
- Demonstrates he was never truly an enemy, just misled

**Chaka:**
- Reveals hidden depths with Devil Fruit powers
- Despite defeat, uses last strength to warn about the bomb
- Embodies loyalty and sacrifice

**Crocodile:**
- At peak ruthlessness and confidence
- Openly mocks his enemies
- Has contingency plans for every scenario
- The quote "I won't bother to fight them since they're going to die anyway" shows his cold pragmatism

**Vivi:**
- Desperately trying to save her kingdom through any means
- Willing to command her army to surrender to save lives
- Witnesses another failure as her peace attempt is sabotaged

### Themes

**Sacrifice and Futility:**
The episode is heavy with the theme of noble sacrifice that proves futile against overwhelming power and lack of knowledge. The Tsumegeri Guards, Chaka, and Koza all make sacrifices, but Crocodile's Logia powers and thorough planning render their efforts ineffective.

**Manipulation vs. Truth:**
The contrast between Crocodile's manipulation and Vivi/Koza's truth-seeking is central. The shooting of Koza shows that even when truth is revealed, manipulation can still prevail through violence.

**Desperation:**
Every character is desperate - the guards desperate to protect their king, Chaka desperate to stop Crocodile, Koza desperate to end the war, Vivi desperate to save her people. This desperation drives the episode's emotional weight.

## Final Thoughts

Episode 120 is a pivotal episode in the Alabasta arc, showcasing the desperation of Alabasta's defenders and the thoroughness of Crocodile's manipulation. The tragic deaths of the Tsumegeri Guards and the shooting of Koza create powerful emotional moments that raise the stakes for the arc's climax.

The episode effectively balances action (Chaka's transformation and fight), tragedy (the guards' deaths), political intrigue (Koza learning the truth), and tension (the bomb timer). It's a strong example of One Piece's ability to juggle multiple narrative threads while maintaining emotional resonance.

The themes of sacrifice, manipulation, and the futility of fighting without proper knowledge are all relevant to One Piece's broader narrative, making this episode important beyond just advancing the Alabasta plot.
