# Research Notes for Episode 26

## Episode Overview
Episode 26 "Zeff and Sanji's Dream! The Illusionary All Blue!" is a pivotal backstory episode that reveals Sanji's childhood and his relationship with Zeff. This episode is highly rated (8.7/10 on IMDb) and is considered one of the most emotional episodes in the Baratie arc.

## Key Research Findings

### Manga vs Anime Differences
The most significant difference between the manga and anime versions concerns how Zeff lost his leg:

**Anime Version:**
- Zeff's leg got caught in ship wreckage during the rescue
- He cut it off with a chain to free himself and save Sanji
- They were stranded for 47 days

**Manga Version (Chapters 56-59):**
- Zeff rescued Sanji with both legs intact
- He later ate his own leg to survive while giving all the food to Sanji
- They were stranded for 85 days
- The conversation happened on the 70th day

**Reason for Change:**
According to Eiichiro Oda in SBS Volume 15, this was changed due to censorship concerns. The idea of Zeff eating his own leg was considered too shocking for young viewers on television.

### Character Development
This episode is crucial for understanding Sanji's character:
1. Establishes his lifelong dream of finding the All Blue
2. Reveals his profound debt to Zeff (why he can't leave Baratie easily)
3. Shows his childhood profession as a cook
4. Demonstrates the shared dream between mentor and student

## Personal Observations

This episode represents a turning point in how One Piece handles backstory. Rather than spreading Sanji's history across multiple episodes, it condenses the essential elements into powerful flashback sequences interwoven with present-day action. This format becomes a template for future backstory episodes.

The censorship change regarding Zeff's leg is interesting from a production standpoint. While the anime version is less graphic, it arguably makes Zeff's sacrifice even more immediate and heroic (risking his life in the moment vs. slow starvation). Both versions effectively convey the depth of his sacrifice.

The All Blue concept introduced here becomes a long-running mystery in One Piece, still unresolved over 1000 episodes later, making this early establishment crucial for Sanji's character arc.
