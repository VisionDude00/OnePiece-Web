## Episode Synopsis

Vivi discovers the bomb hidden in Alubarna's clock tower and defeats Mr. 7 and Miss Father's Day who are guarding it. However, she realizes the bomb cannot be stopped as it has an active timer. In a heroic sacrifice, Pell transforms into his falcon form, grabs the bomb, and flies it high into the sky above Alubarna. As he ascends, he declares himself as Arabasta's Guardian Deity before the bomb explodes, saving the entire kingdom. Meanwhile, Luffy continues his battle against Crocodile and is poisoned by Crocodile's hook, raising the stakes of their final confrontation.

## Notable Findings

### 1. The Pell Controversy
This episode is at the center of one of One Piece's most discussed controversies. Pell's heroic sacrifice appears final and emotionally devastating, but his survival revealed in Episode 130 became highly controversial among fans. This established what fans call the "Pell Effect" - a pattern of seemingly fatal situations that characters survive.

### 2. The 9/11 Theory
A widely circulated but **unverified** theory suggests Oda changed Pell's fate due to sensitivity around the September 11, 2001 attacks. The episode aired September 8, 2002 (about 1 year after 9/11) and features a character performing what appears to be a suicide bombing. **Important**: Oda has never confirmed this theory.

### 3. Animation Quality
While specific sakuga analysis wasn't available, the episode has an IMDb rating of 8.9/10, indicating strong viewer reception. The sacrifice scene is widely considered one of the most emotional moments in the Arabasta Arc.

### 4. Voice Acting
- **Pell (JP)**: Kenji Nojima (initially misidentified as Kenji Nomura in early searches)
- **Pell (EN)**: Kevin M Connolly
- **Vivi (JP)**: Misa Watanabe
- **Vivi (EN)**: Caitlin Glass

### 5. Cultural Impact
The phrase "I am Arabasta's Guardian Deity, The Falcon!" became an iconic line that is frequently referenced in One Piece discussions. The episode sparked ongoing debates about stakes, consequences, and character deaths in the series.
