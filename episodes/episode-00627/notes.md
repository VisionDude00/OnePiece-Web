# Research Notes for Episode 627

## Episode Overview
Episode 627 "Luffy Dies at Sea?! The Pirate Alliance Comes Apart!" is the second episode of the three-episode Caesar Retrieval Arc, a filler arc that takes place between the Punk Hazard Arc and the Dressrosa Arc.

## Key Research Findings

### Filler Arc Context
- This is the 11th filler arc overall and the 2nd filler arc after the timeskip
- Episodes 626-627 are pure filler, while episode 628 contains mixed canon/filler content
- The arc does not appear in the manga at all
- Takes place during the journey from Punk Hazard to Dressrosa

### Air Date Information
- Japanese air date: January 5, 2014
- Never aired on US Toonami (Toonami ended at episode 384 in 2017, resumed at episode 517 in 2022)
- Available on Crunchyroll for streaming

### Opening/Ending Songs
- Opening: "Hands Up!" by Kota Shinzato (episodes 591-628)
- Ending: None (Toei stopped airing ending themes from episode 279 to 1070)
- Note: Funimation simulcast had licensing issues with "Hands Up!" and temporarily used "We Go!" instead

### Character Notes

#### Breed
- Main antagonist of the arc
- Ate the Peto Peto no Mi (Pet-Pet Fruit) - non-canon Paramecia
- Design resembles a ringmaster/circus performer
- Voice actors: Kazuya Ichijo (JP), Oliver Tull (EN)
- First non-canon character to possess a SMILE fruit (though uneaten)

#### Kung-Fu Dugong
- Last appeared in episodes 92-93 during the Alabasta Arc
- Callback spanning 530+ episodes
- Learned Armament Haki during the timeskip
- Formed the Sea Animal Pirates and reached the New World
- Demonstrates how Luffy's influence inspires even minor characters

### Devil Fruit Analysis: Peto Peto no Mi

**Powers:**
- Creates green gel-like collars that control living creatures
- User issues verbal commands that victims must obey
- Works on any living creature regardless of strength
- Can control multiple targets simultaneously
- User can even collar themselves for self-enhancement

**Weaknesses (Critical):**
- Commands must be heard and comprehended
- Cannot control consciousness/thoughts, only body movements
- Earplugs or deafness completely negate the ability
- Collars shatter when user is knocked unconscious
- Standard Devil Fruit weakness (cannot swim)

**Significance:**
Law's discovery of the earplug weakness is the key plot point that enables the alliance's victory in episode 628.

### Plot Connections

#### SMILE Subplot
Breed's plan to use Caesar to mass-produce SMILE fruits connects the filler arc to the main storyline's artificial devil fruit subplot involving:
- Doflamingo's underground operations
- Kaido's army building
- Caesar's scientific capabilities

This makes the arc somewhat more relevant to the ongoing narrative despite being filler.

### Additional Context

This episode represents a relatively rare occurrence in One Piece: a filler arc that directly involves major ongoing plot elements (Caesar and the SMILE trade) while still being completely skippable. The arc's placement between major canon arcs (Punk Hazard and Dressrosa) makes narrative sense but doesn't impact the main storyline.

The Kung-Fu Dugong's return is the most notable element for long-time fans, serving as a reminder of how expansive the One Piece world is and how even minor characters have their own journeys occurring off-screen.
